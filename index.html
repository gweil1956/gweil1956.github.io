Silicon Photonics Design

Y-Junction Splitter

Passive Component

Design and simulation of a silicon-on-insulator Y-junction power splitter. The device achieves near-equal 3 dB splitting across a broad wavelength range through optimized branching angle and taper geometry. FDTD and EME simulations were used to characterize insertion loss, back-reflection, and splitting uniformity.

Edge Coupler

I/O Interface

Inverse-taper edge coupler designed for efficient fiber-to-chip coupling on a silicon photonics platform. The tapered waveguide tip expands the optical mode to match a lensed or cleaved fiber, minimizing mode mismatch loss. Simulations characterize coupling efficiency as a function of taper length, tip width, and fiber alignment tolerance.
